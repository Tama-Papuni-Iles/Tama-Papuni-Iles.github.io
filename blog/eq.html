Emotional Intelligence

What is Emotional Intelligence?
Emotional intelligence is something we are losing in today's modern world, it’s the emotions that are a hidden due to modern day stress and expectancies. Self-awareness and the ability to listen to others with patience are great practices of emotional intelligence. I believe Maori culture is a good example of expressing and engaging with self-emotions and using culture as a good guide to behavior. There is hui (meetings) where ideas are passed around the room until everyone agrees. There is kapahaka where emotions are expressed through waiata-a-ringa, a time for being happy and expressive, moteatea-a time for sadness and grievance, and the haka- a time to let out the anger and frustration, all of which displayed for everyone to see and feel. And then celebrated by a cup of tea with more korero about each other's well-being. I believe it is this structure that acts as a great guidance for your own behavior and emotions and awareness for others how they are feeling, and by this we create a happy mind frame with room for exceedance.

How is it different to IQ?
EQ is self-awareness and detail to what others are feeling, where-as IQ is intelligence without factoring in emotional response. Intelligence is what got us here today and without it we wouldn’t have achieved what we have today, but someone could have a high IQ and lack of EQ which ultimately will show in their work and leadership skills.

Why is it important?
It is very important to keep in mind the true values of EQ if we want to live a balanced life-style and be great leaders. A lack in EQ means we are unaware of what other people, weather they are your workers or peers are feeling, which results in a buildup of uncertainty and lack of confidence. Listening to others and also yourself creates a happy mind frame and confidence to deliver full potential. It will help with happiness and stress and is very sought after in work environments in all industries.
