Self-awareness and the ability to listen to others with patience are great practices of emotional intelligence. I believe Maori culture is a good example of expressing and engaging with self-emotions and using culture as a good guide to behavior. There is hui (meetings) where ideas are passed around the room until everyone agrees. There is kapahaka where emotions are expressed through waiata-a-ringa, a time for being happy and expressive, moteatea-a time for sadness and grievance, and the haka- a time to let out the anger and frustration, all of which displayed for everyone to see and feel. And then celebrated by a cup of tea with more korero about each other's well-being. I believe it is this structure that acts as a great guidance for your own behavior and emotions and awareness for others how they are feeling, and by this we create a happy mind frame with room for exceedance.
